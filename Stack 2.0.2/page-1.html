Founder of G-nome (g-nome.org)

A platform that famously "put genetic data on the blockchain", allowing users to anonymously share genetic data to researcher's datasets. Even though they were anonymous, they could also get paid for their contributions. The platform's technology later moved into ConsenSys.

Pikachai

Brain Computer Interfaces (Sponsored by Microsoft).

Built a mind-controlled robot, brain-controlled MP3 Players, and prosthetics, controlled with EEGs and electrodes. I became sponsored by Microsoft for neurotech development. My brain controlled car won the international grand prize at the Impossible Science Challenge by engineering.com and was demoed on Discovery Channel and other international media.

The Analyzer at IBM

Now, at IBM I started a team to automate the code QA process, a monotonous task that can take hours per day for developers when they write new code. Now, we're building the Analyzer. A tool to analyze call-graphs, C++, and LLVMIR to autonomously generate and run code tests. Now, we’re a team of five full-timers, patent-pending, and on track to save millions of dollars per IBM team with our product.
